The Low Houses consolidation consists of two developments: Glenmore Plaza and the Seth Low Houses. They are both federally funded conventional developments located across the street from each other in the Brownsville section of Brooklyn. \par \vspace{.7\baselineskip}Built in 1968, Glenmore Plaza is a 440-unit, 4.27-acre development with an official population of 793. It consists of four 10-24 story residential buildings, green space, and three playgrounds. \par \vspace{.7\baselineskip}Built in 1967, the Seth Low Houses is a 536-unit, 5.89-acre development with an official population of 1,353. It consists of four 17-18 story residential buildings and four playgrounds.
